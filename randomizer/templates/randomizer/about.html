What is ZORA?

ZORA is a backronym for Zelda One Randomizer for All, a program that generates completely new dungeons and shuffles around a bunch of stuff in The Legend of Zelda, a.k.a. Zelda 1. The world looks the same, the game's objective is the same, but plenty of things can get shuffled around — such as enemies, progression items, and shop items! It's a different experience every time!

Woah cool! Where do I download this?

You don't! ZORA is entirely web-based. You simply provide your original ROM to the site, and it does the rest. No downloading required! Click the Generate Randomized Game link to get started!

Awesome! Can I play with others?

Yes! Join the our discord at https://discord.gg/EdJUSug! If you're interested in speedrunning the original game, there are plenty of resources to help you get started at http://www.redcandle.us!

What does the "for All" in ZORA mean?

Unlike other closed-source randomizers, ZORA is a randomizer that's both created by and created for the Zelda One community.

All interested members of the community are able to:

  • Access the entirity of the ZORA source code.
  • Add features or contribute other content to the randomizer.
  • Actively take a role in decision-making.

Additionally, the community is intended to be welcoming to all people. In particular, the randomizer includes features designed for accessibility, such as a setting to disable bright flashes useful for folks with photosensitivity.

Just who is responsible for this thing?

This program wouldn't have been possible without the work of many people:

  • ZORA was written and is maintained by Tetraly with contributions from Flutterby, Madewokherd, Drascia, Sinistral, Ireneista, and others on ROM hacking, coding work, and playtesting.
  • Huge thanks to fcoughlin for his Zelda Randomizer that provided much of the inspiration for creating this randomizer. Some included features, such as select swap, code/hash generation, removing low-health beeping, were directly adapted from his work.
  • Metalmachine, Fiskbit, and the team behind the Z1M1 randomizer, who helped with the incremental upgrade and fast dungeon scrolling features.
  • A major hat tip to Dormaster Flek for his work on the SMRPG Randomizer web UI, which was blatently copied (with permission) for ZORA.
